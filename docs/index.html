David Akinboro

I'm obsessed with a simple question: can we build AI systems that actually think through problems instead of just pattern-matching their way to answers?

That question drives my research at Cornell Universit, where I'm wrapping up my MS in Computer Science as part of a selective 12-student program. I work with Claire Cardie on reinforcement learning and natural language processing, specifically using RL and efficient test-time scaling to make language models reason more systematically while keeping costs realistic.

But what makes my approach different is, before diving into AI research, I spent time in software engineering and sales. That experience taught me to look beyond academic benchmarks and ask harder questions - like whether a "brilliant" model actually works when real people need to use it in messy, real-world situations.

My focus is building aligned reasoning systems that are both transparent and trustworthy. Because what's the point of breakthrough research if it never makes it out of the lab to help actual people solve actual problems?

Current Research

Making AI Show Its Work

Most legal AI is a black box - you get answers but no explanation of how it got there. I'm building what I call "glass box" legal AI using attention analysis and interpretability techniques. Every conclusion the system makes can be traced back to specific facts and evidence. It's like having an AI that can highlight exactly which sentences influenced its reasoning.

Building AI You Can Actually Trust

My other project addresses the bigger question: how do you deploy AI in high-stakes situations like legal analysis or medical diagnosis? I'm developing a multi-stage system where each reasoning step has built-in safety checks and human oversight points. The goal isn't just accurate AI - it's AI that professionals can confidently rely on when it matters.
