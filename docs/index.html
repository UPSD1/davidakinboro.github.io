Current Research

🔍 Glass Box AI: Making Black Boxes Transparent

Most legal AI systems provide their answers without traceable explainability, which is fine for consumer apps, but ungrounded for professionals who need to stake their reputation on the reasoning behind it.

As a spin off from the legalReasoner project, we decided to commit ourselves to research what we call "glass box" legal AI systems where every conclusion can be traced back to specific evidence. Using attention analysis and interpretability techniques, you can see exactly which facts and legal principles influenced each decision. It's like having an AI research assistant that can highlight its sources and explain its logic.

The technical challenge isn't just accuracy it's building trust. Legal professionals need to understand not just what the AI concluded, but how it got there and when they should question it.

🛡️ Multi-Stage Safety: AI That Knows Its Limits

Here’s the real problem with deploying AI in high-stakes domains: most systems hallucinate. They'll confidently give you the wrong answer about a judicial reasoning or legal precedent.

We are developing a multi-stage approach with built-in safety checks and human oversight points. Instead of one big AI making all the decisions, you get a system that breaks complex reasoning into steps, flags uncertainty, and hands off to humans when it hits its limits.

The goal isn't just increasing accuracy but having an AI system that professionals can confidently rely on because it's honest about what it knows and what it doesn't.
